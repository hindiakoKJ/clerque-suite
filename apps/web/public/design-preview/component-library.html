Three sizes — cashier (64dp · primary actions in the sales flow), default (48dp · most everything), compact (36dp · in-row actions). Four variants × four states.

Default · 48dp · 16px · flat

All other primary actions outside the sales flow — saving settings, closing a shift, confirming a void. Lives in modals, dialogs, headers.

Compact · 36dp · 14px

In-row actions: re-print receipt, retry sync, dismiss a chip. Below 36dp is too small for a thumb on a 10.5" tablet.

6-digit variant · supervisor / refund authorization

Solo Standard + tier exposes a supervisor PIN for void / refund authorization. The 6-digit variant uses tighter dot spacing (12dp vs 16dp) so a single hand can cover the row when entering. Never use 8 digits — anything longer than 6 trains users to look away from the screen to remember the digits, which is the opposite of safe.

Toast · snackbar

Bottom-anchored on tablet (above the cart panel), bottom-center on phone. Auto-dismiss at 4s for info, 6s when an action is offered, sticky for errors that require acknowledgement.

Variants

✓ Payment confirmedRef 0xA12B · ₱288.00 received via GCash · Sukli ₱0.00 View
Neutral · low-emphasis ack
i Tier limit reached — recipes locked at 5. Manage at clerque.com.

Behavior

Position — tablet landscape: bottom-left of the product grid (clear of the cart panel); phone portrait: bottom-center, above the system nav bar.
Stacking — only one snackbar at a time. A new snack replaces the old with a slide-up transition.
Duration — info 4s, with-action 6s, error sticky (must be dismissed via action or swipe).
Dismissal — swipe down or tap action. No "×" button — the snack is meant to feel ambient, not modal.
Not used for — confirming a payment (that's the receipt screen), prompting an action (that's a button), errors requiring re-entry (that's an inline field error).
